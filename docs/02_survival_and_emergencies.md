# 02. Survival, Logistics, and Emergencies

Mastering this core vocabulary will allow you to survive your first few days in Bengaluru. These aren't just words; they are the communicative tools you need to negotiate, direct transport, fulfill basic needs, and handle critical situations.

## 1. The Binary Engine of Needs (Want vs. Don't Want)
In a new city, clearly expressing your desires and boundaries is critical.

*   **Bēku (Want / Need):** The absolute essential. Use it to request items or express necessity.
    *   *Usage:* "Nanage neeru **bēku**" (I want water).
*   **Bēda (Don't want / Don't need):** Your shield against unwanted offers, higher fares, or persistent vendors. 
    *   *Usage:* "Plastic bag **bēda**" (No plastic bag). "One-and-half **bēda**" (I won't pay 1.5x meter).

## 2. Establishing Reality (Presence and Knowledge)
These words help you determine if something exists or if you understand a situation.

*   **Illa (No / Not here):** A powerful word indicating non-existence, absence, or direct disagreement.
    *   *Usage:* "Neeru **illa**" (There is no water).
*   **Gothilla (I don't know):** Highly useful for establishing boundaries regarding local knowledge or language limits.
    *   *Usage:* "Nanage Kannada **gothilla**" (I don't know Kannada).

## 3. The Commands of Motion (Verbs)
Always remember the hierarchy of respect. Add an `-i` or `-ri` to the verb root to be polite to strangers and service personnel.

*   **Banni (Come):** The respectful imperative of *Baa*. Essential for requesting someone's presence.
*   **Hogi (Go):** The respectful imperative of *Hogu*. Crucial for directing transport.
*   **Nillisi (Stop):** The standard respectful command for instructing an auto or cab driver to halt.

## 4. Navigating the Streets (Directions)

*   **Mundhe (Forward / Ahead):** Used to indicate forward motion.
*   **Hindhe (Behind / Back):** Indicates the rear or reversing direction.
*   **Balagade (Right side):** Made of *Bala* (Right) and *gade* (Side).
*   **Edagade (Left side):** Made of *Eda* (Left) and *gade* (Side).
*   **Nera (Straight):** A directional adverb used extensively in navigation.

## 5. Transactions and Giving

*   **Esht-aithu? (How much did it become?):** The critical phrase for all financial transactions and market inquiries.
*   **Kodi (Give):** The respectful imperative used when requesting items in a shop or asking for a ticket/change.
*   **Thagonli (Take):** Use this respectfully when handing cash or items to a vendor.
*   **Swalpa kami maadi (Reduce a little):** The standard, polite mechanism for initiating bargaining in a market.
*   **Bidi (Leave / Let go):** Tell the vendor to keep the change, or use it to drop a subject.

## 6. Communication Actions

*   **Heli (Tell / Say):** Used to prompt information. *"Kannadadalli heli"* (Say it in Kannada).
*   **Kalsi (Send):** The respectful imperative. *"Location kalsi"* (Send the location).
*   **Haki (Put / Pour):** Used physically (pouring coffee) and mechanically *"Meter haki"*.
*   **Maadi (Do):** The universal auxiliary verb. Attach it to any English noun to create a 'Kanglish' verb (e.g., "Google Pay maadi").

## 7. Dining and Restaurants

| Kannada | English Meaning | Context / Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Oota Aitha?** | Had lunch/dinner? | Universal greeting after "Hello". |
| **Menu kodi** | Give menu | Asking for the menu respectfully. |
| **By-two coffee kodi** | Give shared coffee | Ordering one coffee split into two glasses (A Bangalore classic). |
| **Bill kodi** | Give bill | Finishing the meal. |
| **Kara kadi madi**| Make it less spicy | Very useful instruction for restaurants. |
| **Oota chennagide**| Food is good | *"Dosa chennagide."* (Dosa is good). |

## 8. Household Emergencies

| Kannada | English Meaning | Context / Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Neeru barthilla** | Water is not coming | Critical plumbing issue. |
| **Current illa** | No power | Reporting a power cut. |
| **Clean maadi** | Clean it | *"Room clean maadi."* (Clean the room). |
| **Naale banni** | Come tomorrow | *"Ivathu beda, naale banni."* (Not today, come tomorrow). |

## 9. Boundary Setting and Conflict
Be extremely careful with these phrases. They are blunt and direct.

| Kannada | English Meaning | Context / Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nannige help beku**| I need help | Emergency situation calling for assistance. |
| **Hogo! / Hogi!** | Go! / Get lost! | *Hogo* is a rude dismissal. *Hogi* is the respectful "Please go". |
| **Police kares-thini**| I will call police | A direct threat for severe harassment. |
| **Nanna thappu aaythu**| My mistake happened | The safest way to apologize to authority or police. |
