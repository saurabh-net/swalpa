# 05. Sprint 4: Time, Numbers & Calendar

Mastering the numbers 1 through 10 unlocks the logic for the entire numerical system. The days of the week match Hindi exactly, as both are derived from Sanskrit planetary names.

## 1. Core Numerical Logic
Learning the base numbers allows you to manage fares, quantities, and addresses. 

*   **0:** Sonne (*Hindi: Shunya* - note the shared root)
*   **1:** Ondu
*   **2:** Eradu
*   **3:** Mooru
*   **4:** Naalku
*   **5:** Aidhu
*   **6:** Aaru
*   **7:** Yelu
*   **8:** Entu
*   **9:** Ombathu
*   **10:** Hattu. Notice the shift from the ancient root *Pattu*.

### The 'Teens' and Multiples of Ten

*   **11:** Hannondu (Hattu + Ondu).
*   **12:** Hanneradu (Hattu + Eradu).
*   **20:** Ippathu. The '-thu' ending denotes a multiple of ten.
*   **30:** Muvathu.
*   **40:** Nalavathu.
*   **50:** Aivathu (Based on 5/Aidhu).
*   **60:** Aruvathu (Based on 6/Aaru).
*   **100:** Nooru. Vital for larger transactions and rent discussions.
*   **1000:** Saavira.
*   **Lakh:** Laksha. (*Hindi: Laakh*)

## 2. Fractions and Measurements
Essential for buying vegetables or telling precise time.

*   **Ardha (Half):** 
    *   *Usage:* "Ardha gante" (Half hour).
    *   *Hindi Parallel:* Aadha.
*   **Kaalu (Quarter):** 
    *   *Usage:* "Moora-kaalu" (3:15 / Three and a quarter).
*   **Muk-kaalu (3/4th):** 
    *   *Usage:* "Mooru-muk-kaalu" (3:45).
## 3. Time and Scheduling Logistics
These nouns and adverbs allow you to coordinate meetings and set expectations.

*   **Samaya (Time):** Noun used to ask duration or exact clock time.
    *   *Usage:* "Samaya eshtu?" (What is the time?)
*   **Gante (Hour):** 
    *   *Usage:* "Ondu gante" (1 o'clock or 1 hour duration).
*   **Nimisha (Minute):** 
    *   *Usage:* "Hattu nimisha" (10 minutes).
*   **Eega (Now):** Temporal adverb indicating the present moment.
    *   *Usage:* "Eega banni" (Come right now).
*   **Aamele (Later):** Extremely common in delaying tactics or scheduling.
    *   *Usage:* "Aamele banni" (Come later).

### Days of the Week
The days of the week share identical Sanskrit roots with Hindi. Add "-vaara".

*   Sunday: Bhanu-vaara
*   Monday: Soma-vaara
*   Tuesday: Mangala-vaara
*   Wednesday: Budha-vaara

*   Thursday: Guru-vaara
*   Friday: Shukra-vaara
*   Saturday: Shani-vaara

### The Daily Calendar

*   **Beligge (Morning):** "Beligge 8 gante" (Morning 8 AM).
*   **Madhyana (Afternoon):** "Madhyana oota" (Afternoon lunch).
*   **Sanje (Evening):** "Sanje coffee" (Evening coffee).
*   **Rathri (Night):** "Rathri oota" (Dinner).
*   **Ivathu (Today):** "Ivathu raje ide" (Today is a holiday).
*   **Naale (Tomorrow):** "Naale sigona" (Let's meet tomorrow).
*   **Ninne (Yesterday):** "Ninne bandru" (Came yesterday).
*   **Nadiddu (Day after tomorrow):** "Nadiddu banni" (Come the day after).
