# 08. Sprint 7: Bangalore Slang & Kanglish

Bengaluru spoken Kannada is a highly dynamic and syncretic dialect. It seamlessly blends *Hosagannada* (Modern Kannada) with English (forming "Kanglish"), incorporates loanwords from neighboring languages (like Tamil), and features a rich tapestry of local street slang. 

Mastering this localized vocabulary is essential for social integration and daily logistics.

## 1. The Hierarchy of Respect and Brotherhood
Bangaloreans value linguistic politeness, but also have distinct terms for close peers.

| Kanglish / Slang | Literal Meaning | Bangalore Context and Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Maga / Macha** | Son / Brother-in-law | Universal term for a close male friend or peer. ("Bro/Dude"). *Macha* is a Tamil loanword very common in Bangalore. |
| **Guru / Boss** | Teacher / Leader | Universal term of address for auto drivers, waiters, or strangers. |
| **Sisya** | Student | A protégé, sidekick, or younger pal. *"Nanna sisya avnu."* (He's my pal). |
| **Oota aytha?** | Food done? | The primary social icebreaker used immediately after hello. *"Oota aytha guru?"* (Had food, boss?). |
| **Yen guru, yen samachar?** | What boss, what news? | Highly casual greeting among peers to establish immediate friendly rapport. |

## 2. Kanglish and The "Maadi" Engine
Kanglish effortlessly integrates English nouns and verbs into Kannada. This is most commonly achieved by appending a euphonic `-u` to English nouns (e.g., "Car-u", "Office-u") or attaching the universal action verb *maadi* (do) to English verbs.

| Kanglish Term | Meaning | Bangalore Context and Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Adjust maadi** | Please accommodate | A cultural cornerstone. Used to ask for physical space on a bus, a favor, or a compromise. *"Swalpa adjust maadi."* |
| **Drop kodi** | Give a lift | *"Metro hathira drop kodi."* (Drop me near the metro). |
| **Scene illa** | No chance / No issue | Used to dismiss a problem. *"Eno problem? Scene illa."* |

## 3. Street Descriptors (The Good and the Bad)

| Slang Term | English Meaning | Bangalore Context and Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bombaat / Sakkath**| Fantastic / Super | Used to describe anything of excellent quality (food, movies). *"Movie sakkath agithu"* |
| **Chindi** | Shredded / Amazing| Describes a performance or object so good it destroyed the competition. |
| **Dabba** | Tin box / Useless | Completely useless, broken, or of extremely low quality (*Dabba service*). |
| **Kirik** | Trouble | A nuisance, an argument, or an irritating person. *"Avnu thumba kirik."* |
| **Gand-u** | Strong / Cool | Context matters. Can mean "Boss style" or "Macho" (not abusive in this specific context). |
| **Full-tight** | Drunk | *"Avnu full tight agidane."* (He is completely drunk). |
| **Off-aago** | Get lost / Shut up | A rude dismissal. *"Summane off aago."* (Just quiet down/get lost). |
